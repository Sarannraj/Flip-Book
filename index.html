PAPAYA

The Portuguese introduced papaya to India in the late 16th century, through Malaysia and the Philippines.

Papayas contain high levels of antioxidants such as vitamin A, Vitamin C,and vitamin E.Diets high in antioxidants may reduce the risk of heart disease.The antioxidants prevent the oxidation of cholesterol.

PINEAPPLE

According to the National Horticulture Board, the Portuguese introduced pineapple cultivation to India in 1548 AD.

Pineapple is high in vitamin C, which helps your immune system -- The body's defense against germs -- keep you healthy. It also has: Vitamins A, B6, E, and K. Calcium.

POMEGRANATE

The portuguese introduced pineapples to India in the 1550s. however, some say that pineapple was grown in India during the maurya period (322 to 185 BC) and the Gupta Empire (320 to 550 AD)

Pomegranate contains many nutrients and is a good source of: Vitamin E, magnesium, which helps manage blood pressure and glucose levels.
